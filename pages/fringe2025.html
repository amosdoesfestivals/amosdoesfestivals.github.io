Fringe Shows 2025

My name is Amos - I am a university professor, an Edinburgh resident and love the Festivals. Home is the best place to be in the whole world in August :).

These brief show reviews (mostly fringe, but sometimes others) are provided as people keep asking for them and seem to find them useful. A great play differs from a bad one, but these reviews are all personal opinion, do not indicate any sort of agreement with any performance, and your mileage may vary. Together with my wife, we have been festivalling for over a quarter of a century, so we have become fairly good at picking what is likely to have a chance of being good, but I still try to throw in randomness to try to prevent me just reinforcing my biases.

In general, I find good plays and some physical theatre more long-term memorable and reflective than comedy (with some exceptions), though I do enjoy a good comic.

The order here is my ranking. My star ratings are designed to match the usual expectations for such things (maybe a bit harsher than some), but I also provide a more fine-tuned rating. These are: (1*) awful, (1*) bad, (2*) poor, (3*, but wouldn't go again in a Groundhog Day) okay, (3* but might choose to redo) fair, (4*) good, (4*) very good, (5* - only a few a year) excellent, (5* - one a year maybe and will never forget this show) outstanding.

So what was best this year? Well, in the end, we have to admit that it was the weather. I'm used to dodging raindrops during an Edinburgh Fringe August, but this year has been incredible. At times I even declined to go to shows as the weather was too good to descend into the dark.
